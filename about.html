Our Story

Warning signs, especially in the last decade, like the heat waves, the rapid rate of ozone depletion, the bushfires, the earthquakes, droughts and tsunamis were enough stimulus for us to start working in order to secure our environment and minimise the unforeseen horrors.
We started our endeavor by organising plantation drives where we carry out tree plantations, tend to them, and undertake the necessary steps for their maintenance. After the overwhelming success of our plantation drives, the FORENGERS took it upon themselves to organize cleanliness drives. These drives focus on a particular area with saturated waste and is cleaned by the vigilant group members with proper equipment and sanitation.
When the pandemic of COVID-19 hit us all, binding us to our homes, plantation and cleanliness drives were out of bounds, but we refused to stop. We came up with 3 different initiatives to keep up their good work. We started making newspaper bags, cloth bags out of old waste T-shirts and Newspaper Envelopes for safe disposal of sanitary products.

Plantation drive

Plantation drive encourages the younger generation to plant more trees and also guides them in the process. Till date we have carried out 21 plantation drives in which we have planted above 250 saplings. We follow a standard procedure for all the plantation drives:
First, we look for a perfect location to carry out the drive. We prefer open areas or forest areas like Taljai hill and ARAI, plantation in such areas helps the habitat of the wildlife there. Permissions are taken from the respective authority to carry out the drive. Saplings are bought according to the specific habitat. We prefer Indian species of plants for example: Peepal, banyan, etc. We request the members who possess any plantation tools to carry them along during the plantation drive. We buy the tools in bulk for the members who don’t have them. For every drive the members are instructed about the process of plantation, so that the saplings planted by us can be easily identified and taken care of.

Maintenance and Watering Drive

We at Forengers believe that planting trees is not sufficient their growth is also important. The ratio between planting trees and their being alive should be maintained, so we execute time to time maintenance and watering drives. The primary task of maintenance is tagging the saplings. This helps us to identify the saplings and keep their record. The tags are prepared using the waste material. Some saplings require support while growing, which is provided using a bamboo. Regular checking of these supports is done during these drives. At some location’s saplings need to be protected from grazing animals by installing green sheds.
Watering is the most essential part of maintenance. There are regular watering drives that are conducted every week. Members are allotted particular day in a week to carry out this task. We see to it that every plant is watered regularly to ensure consistent growth. Drips are also installed for some plants. These drips are made reusing the plastic bottles collected during the cleanliness drives

Cleanliness drive

We also carry out cleanliness drives. These drives are carried out in a location where a cleanup is needed. Such cleanliness drives need to be carried out with utmost safety, so during our drives the members are instructed about the safety norms they should follow and also the method in which the waste should be collected. We urge all the members to wear proper safety gears like shoes, gloves and mask. All the waste is collected in green garbage bags and is segregated while collecting, which is then handed over to the PMC garbage collectors in that area. During every cleanliness drive more than 15 bags of waste and more than 30 kgs of alcohol bottles are collected.

Paper bags and envelopes

To make the best use of the lockdown, we encouraged the members to make paper bags and sanitary pads disposing envelopes at home using newspapers. This initiative was carried out adhering to the rules of lockdown strictly. Every member was sent a video with the process to make the bags and envelopes and was strictly instructed to make them staying at home. Till now more than 1200 paper bags and 600 envelopes are made. These items will be donated to the vendors, who still use plastic bags to give up on plastic.
We also collected papers which were used on one side and have a scope to be reused. These papers are used to make stickers to put on the paper bags and envelopes. Handmade posters informing about the drive are also made using these papers.
